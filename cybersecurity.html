Cybersecurity

What does it do?

Cybersecurity is the protection of electronic data and other assets from unauthorised use and the practices and methods used to do this. In today’s world, a wealth of information is stored digitally, and these digital assets may be personal and confidential. Criminals may try to gain access to and obtain this data for financial gain or other malicious intents. This is why cybersecurity has become such an important technology in IT in recent years. An eternal arms race exists between users trying to breach security measures to illegally access data and cybersecurity experts and ends users trying to protect their data. With the ever-increasing use of devices which are connected to networks, convenience and quality of life has improved greatly but this convenience of being connected also brings the risk of making your data vulnerable. This is why cybersecurity has become so necessary in today’s world to give us peace of mind to preserve our way of life.

There are five distinct categories of cybersecurity: critical infrastructure security, application security, network security, cloud security and Internet of Things (IoT) security. All these areas must be considered when developing a cybersecurity plan. In addition to this, there are three key components which play an active role when it comes to cybersecurity: people, processes, and technology. The human aspect of this is the easiest for most people to contribute to. This focuses on what people can do themself to better protect themselves from cybersecurity threats. This includes using stronger passwords and password idleness, visiting suspicious websites, and sharing sensitive personally identifiable information on social media. Backing up data and limiting physical access of your devices is also recommended. Processes refers to systems put in place to detect and identify threats to respond and recover from them accordingly. Technology comprises of the technological tools that are used to protect your information and devices like firewalls, DNS filtering and malware protection.

Cybersecurity, like the networks and data storage solutions that they protect are an ever-evolving technology. It needs to adapt to recent technologies and new threats and exploits what are constantly found and abused. It will keep becoming more complex as systems become more complex with multiple layers of protection required. Currently, a wide array of methods is used to make sure that only authorised users can access the information they are meant to see. This includes passwords and pin codes, two-factor authentication, software to protect devices against malware and phishing attempts to name a few. This is used to protect things ranging from email accounts to banking and other highly sensitive information. Businesses now also invest heavily in cybersecurity to protect confidential data in an age where almost all data is stored digitally and can be accessed from anywhere in the world. Development of new codes and software needs to be constantly created and monitored to combat malicious attacks. This along with the creation of more secure devices and most importantly cybersecurity awareness and education is key to protecting and preventing information from being used and stolen by unauthorised users.

What is the likely impact?

Cybersecurity is a necessary technology in this modern world as more information is stored digitally for increased accessibility and the world and its devices and information sharing and communication through digital means has become the norm. It must keep up with constant threats of malicious activity and be a primary consideration when developing new software and systems. The people that will be most affected by this are everyone that lives in modern society. Everyday things that we currently take for granted may be exploited if security is not kept in mind. This includes mobile devices, email, internet banking and credit cards as well as personal information that is stored by the government and other corporations what you have dealings with. A recent example of this is the security breaches of Optus and Medibank. This data breach has impacted many people where their personal information held by those companies was accessed by criminals.

As reliance on technology use continues to increase, many jobs will be created to help secure networks and prevent data loss. There has been an explosion of demand for cybersecurity experts as more data is being moved online and accessibility continues to increase. Few jobs will be made redundant due to this technology as currently, most information is already stored digitally and not as a hardcopy. Current workers in this field will need to continue and grow their skills to keep up with the ever-changing digital landscape.

How will this affect you?

In my daily life, although not always at the forefront of my mind, cybersecurity is always a concern. It keeps all the data I need to go on with my daily life with convenience secure. With the devices I use, I can manage everything from finances to education and healthcare with a few taps on a screen or keyboard. This convenience does come at a cost as if I can access it, others may also try to do so. In such a case, the results could range from being a minor inconvenience to disastrous. Your finances may be under threat, identities may be stolen with accounts being open unknowingly under your name, sensitive information may be accessed among a lengthy list of other things. This is why cybersecurity is important to me and should be important to everyone. As security measures continue to be created and updated, it will give me more peace of mind that my data is secure. In the meantime, I firmly believe that everyone has the personal responsibility to do their best to make sure that they take their personal digital security seriously.
