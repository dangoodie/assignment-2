Autonomous Vehicles

What does it do?

An autonomous vehicle is a self-driving vehicle that uses sensors and software to navigate through its surroundings and environment to operate without any human input or involvement. Currently, there are various levels of automation that a vehicle may have ranging from minimal human input to fully autonomous. Most of these vehicles currently require human input or intervention in emergency situations or when the system cannot act appropriately. As a newly emerging technology, there have been many advances within the past years although due to safety concerns, moral issues, and lack of infrastructure to support this technology, progress toward a fully autonomous vehicle may still be q uite some while away. Due to the nature of this technology, as it involves physical interaction with the physical environment, safety is of utmost importance as any errors may cause harm, injury, or death. As a result, it will take quite some time for this technology to reach maturity.

Several vehicle manufacturers currently offer vehicles with a great deal of driver assistance or partial automation including Tesla and Mercedes Benz, among others. The systems in this vehicle take control of accelerating, braking, and steering but require the user to monitor the driving and intervene when a response is required. These systems currently only work in certain areas where the area has been carefully mapped out and approval from the government granted. Commercially, automated vehicles have been tested in the public transport, taxi services, and freight transportation industries. The trucking industry has a great interest in this technology as it not only makes transportation safer, but also a large amount of fuel can be saved through a technique known a platooning which uses a convoy of trucks following closely together to reduce air resistance for better fuel economy.

As advances in this field continue, automated systems will be further refined with additional testing. The complexity of this technology means that there will be many obstacles to overcome and supporting infrastructure to be placed in the pursuit of a truly autonomous vehicle. The development of Artificial Intelligence (AI) for this system is crucial as it will allow these vehicles to make the best decisions when they experience unexpected encounters such as roadworks, pedestrians, cyclists, and wildlife. More advanced cameras, sensors, and supporting software will also be required to help these vehicles navigate the physical environment. High-definition maps with continuous updates, developments in vehicular communication with peer-to-peer networks, and modifications in current road infrastructure will also be needed to support this system.

What is the likely impact?

The development and perfection of autonomous vehicles will significantly impact on all people in human society. Without the need to focus on controlling a vehicle and giving attention to the surrounding environment, people can dedicate themselves to more important tasks while they commute. This may not only include being more productive with work and other tasks, but also more relaxation and leisure time. If applied correctly, road safety will be improved with more advanced AI and vehicular communication working together to reduce accidents and help traffic flow more efficiently. This would result in a potentially safer and faster commute. Advances in vehicular communication may also eliminate the need for traffic signals which would result in an even more efficient traffic system. One of the main safety concerns will be for pedestrians as they must now rely on AI with advanced sensors to be able to avoid them. One solution to this problem would be to create infrastructure which would eliminate the need for pedestrians to be on the road such as overhead walkways and bridges.

In terms of job creation, this emerging technology is a double-edged sword. People currently employed as a driver in a vehicle such as rideshare/taxi drivers and truck drivers among others will no longer be necessary and will need to seek other alternatives for employment. However, on the positive note, I believe more jobs will be created than lost with this technology. A large workforce will be required to create, update, and maintain autonomous vehicles and the infrastructure and industries that will be necessary to support this technology. This would lead people into more creative and inventive tasks as AI handles the lesser daily activities.

How will this affect you?

In the current state, this IT technology has minor impact on my daily life as the technology is still in its infancy and as a result, is not widely used, especially in Australia. The current benefits of this technology are the supporting technologies which will be required, with further development, for a truly autonomous vehicle such as cruise control, cameras, and various sensors and driver aids in found current vehicles.

Once this technology reaches maturity, it will have a profound impact on my daily life along with the people that surround me and all of society. It would grant us something that we are all sorely lacking in this world: more time. Instead of focus and concentration directed at navigation when commuting, I could better use this time for work, study, leisure, or even getting more rest. The efficiency of all these components and systems of autonomous vehicles working together would also mean smoother traffic flow resulting in less time spent commuting, freeing up even more time for more important things. This efficiency would hopefully also mean that less traffic accidents would occur resulting in a safer world.
